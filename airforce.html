Indian Air Force Pilot

To become an Indian Air Force (IAF) pilot, candidates must meet the following eligibility criteria:

  • Age: Between 16.5 to 19.5 years for NDA entry, and up to 25 years for graduates.
  • Education: Must have passed 10+2 with Physics and Mathematics for NDA entry, and a Bachelor's degree for graduates.
  • Nationality: Must be a citizen of India.
  • Physical Standards: Must meet prescribed physical and medical standards.
  • Marital Status: Unmarried (for certain entries).

Aspiring IAF pilots are required to appear for the following examinations:

  • National Defence Academy (NDA) Exam: Conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC).
  • Combined Defence Services (CDS) Exam: Also conducted by UPSC for graduates.
  • Engineering Knowledge Test (EKT): For engineering graduates applying for technical branches.
  • Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT): For graduates and postgraduates for flying and ground duty branches.

Being an IAF pilot offers numerous advantages, including:

  • Exciting and challenging career opportunities.
  • Opportunity to fly advanced aircraft and participate in missions.
  • Competitive pay and benefits.
  • Training and skill development in aviation technology.
  • Pride in serving the nation and contributing to national security.

IAF pilots may be assigned to various duty types, including:

  • Combat Flying: Engaging in aerial combat and air defense missions.
  • Transport and Logistics: Transporting troops, supplies, and equipment.
  • Airborne Surveillance: Conducting reconnaissance and surveillance operations.
  • Search and Rescue: Participating in search and rescue missions during emergencies.
  • Training and Instruction: Training new pilots and conducting flight instruction.
